# Smouldering Marginalia

A computational philosophy for things that are almost not there. The system models a room where light has weight and ink has memory: motes of dust hold a charge of warmth they slowly spend, and fragments of handwriting detach from a page no one can see and drift upward like the last smoke off a snuffed candle. Nothing in this system is born complete and nothing dies abruptly — every entity is a function of its own age, rising on a single bell-shaped envelope from nothing, through presence, back to nothing, so the canvas always looks inhabited and never looks busy.

Three independent fields share one noise-driven air. A slow vector field, sampled from layered Perlin noise advancing in time, carries the dust on near-imperceptible currents — never fast enough to read as wind, only enough to register, after a held gaze, as proof the room is not a still image. A second, coarser noise field drifts a handful of vast soft-edged luminance pools across the frame, the visual residue of candlelight scattered through unseen incense haze, rendered as true radial gradients rather than stacked primitives so the falloff reads as light and not as shape. The third process is generative ductus: each calligraphic fragment is not text but the *gesture* of text, a tapered Bézier spine built from a small seeded grammar of baseline curvature, ascender count, and diacritic placement — the same economy of marks that lets four pen-strokes and a dot mean a whole word, stripped here of the word and left holding only the gesture.

Craftsmanship in this piece is measured in restraint, not density. The trail buffer is tuned to a precise decay so that every glow leaves exactly enough afterimage to feel like persistence of vision and not motion blur — a constant that took painstaking calibration against frame rate to land on the single value that reads as held breath rather than smear. Stroke weight along every calligraphic spine is sampled continuously rather than set per-segment, so tapering from a Bézier's tangent reads as a true pen lifting off paper, the kind of detail that separates a meticulously crafted generative ductus from a wobbling random line. Twinkle phase, drift heading, and lifespan are each drawn from their own independent noise stream so that, even at high entity counts, no two motes or fragments ever fall into visible lock-step — the mark of an algorithm tuned by someone who has spent real time looking at what large-N independent random-phase systems do wrong, and corrected for it.

Color is not decoration here but a budget. Every entity draws from the same four-hue palette — a near-black ground, a deep green, a warm gold, and a parchment near-white — and the only variable is how much of that budget an entity has earned at its current age. Nothing is ever drawn at full saturation; brightness is itself the envelope, so the same four hex values produce a field that reads as endlessly varied simply because no two entities are ever sampling the envelope at the same phase. This is the discipline of a limited palette pushed to its absolute edge through timing rather than through adding more colors — the harder and more expert path, and the only one that keeps a four-hour loop from ever feeling like it is repeating itself.

The whole system is built to run behind something else and lose every fight for attention on purpose. It is tuned for the gaze that is not on it: peripheral, ambient, ten percent of the eye at most. Every parameter that governs density, speed, and glow was therefore chosen by removing, not adding — the test for "is this finished" was never "is there enough happening" but "can I take one more thing out and still feel the room breathe." That is the harder craft, and the only one this philosophy asks for.
